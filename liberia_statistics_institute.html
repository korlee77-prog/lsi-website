Why Choose LSI

A Center of Statistical Excellence

We combine rigorous academic training with practical data skills to prepare students for careers in government, industry, and research.

Research-Led Learning

All courses are informed by active research in applied statistics, econometrics, and national survey methodology — ensuring relevance to real-world problems.

Statistical Computing Labs

State-of-the-art computer labs equipped with R, SAS, SPSS, Python, and STATA, offering hands-on training in modern analytical software.

National & Regional Impact

We collaborate with LISGIS, World Bank, AfDB, and UN agencies to deliver data solutions that directly inform Liberia's development policies.

Expert Faculty

Learn from experienced statisticians, econometricians, and data scientists with PhDs from leading global universities and decades of applied experience.

Accredited Programs

Our degree programs are fully accredited by Liberia's National Commission on Higher Education with international recognition across West Africa.

Industry Partnerships

Strong ties with banks, telecom companies, NGOs, and the government create robust internship and job placement pipelines for graduates.

Mr. Henry Hali Korlee
Founder & Executive Director, Liberia Statistics Institute

Mr. Henry Hali Korlee is a distinguished statistician, educator, and civic leader who established the Liberia Statistics Institute with a singular vision: to make statistical literacy a cornerstone of Liberia's national development. A lecturer in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at the University of Liberia, he simultaneously pursues postgraduate studies in Statistics at the University of Botswana and Financial Engineering at WorldQuant University — embodying the lifelong learning ethos he instills in every LSI student.

Beyond academia, he serves as CEO of Henria Printing Press and Executive Director of the Sameria Institute of Technology, and leads the development of civic technology infrastructure for Liberia's National Elections Commission in preparation for the 2029 General Elections. His research spans time series analysis, Bayesian inference, survey sampling, and applied econometrics — with a deep commitment to using data science for African development.

BSc Mathematics, University of Liberia MA Regional Science, University of Liberia MA Statistics (candidate), University of Botswana MScFE (candidate), WorldQuant University Lecturer, University of Liberia NEC Civic Tech Lead, Liberia 2029
